Pizza Delivery App - Smartwatch - PizzaHUT

ABOUT THE PROTOTYPE

The prototype was implemented in Figma environment. The colors in the prototype have been chosen to match the company's colors (logos). Its basic task is the ability to remotely place and pay for orders using a smartwatch, but along with this feature is also the option to resume your favorite order, repeat your order from the past, track your order, navigate to the nearest restaurant, remotely enter a location based on GPS . You can place an order for a maximum of 2 products in the prototype. Inaccessible products have been blurred (3 are available). Almost all components are clickable, those that do not add anything to the prototype - their functionality can be seen elsewhere in the prototype. With some functionalities, the displayed content is incorrect (e.g. changing the price with increasing the quantity of the product) but it does not affect the functionality.

BRIEF

1. Reason / Initial Situation / background design

As a company producing and selling pizza, we want to reach a new, wealthy group of recipients by using a new sales channel to increase the volume of pizza sales. Sales of smartwatch devices increase year-on-year by 40% (according to data Canalys 2019), which makes it a promising market.

2. Goals and gauges

The basic goal is to create a working, functional solution on WearOS (Android) and WatchOS (Apple), which does not require additional pairings / synchronization. Indicators (KPI) based on which the success of the solution in Poland will be determined:
- Installation of a minimum of 5000 applications on Apple Watch (AppStore) and Android Watch (Google Play) monthly within 4 quarters
- Aggregated (iOS, Android) 2000 MAU (Monthly Active Users - definition) after 6 months of solution operation
- 1000 orders per week for a minimum of PLN 30 carried out using the smartwatch channel.
- The intermediate goal of the product is to create Pizza Hut as a modern and flexible brand that adapts to the needs of its customers.

3. Brand / product / industry

The PizzaHut brand operates on the gastronomy market in the casual dining and delivery segment. The competitive advantage of the brand is a large number of products, tailored to different target groups, aggressive discount policy and intensive marketing campaigns. Market leader.

Customers that we want to reach as part of the new product are young, active, mobile customers in the 15-24 and 25-35 groups who know the brand well.

6. Past activities

We currently have solutions for ordering food using websites and a mobile application for smartphones (iOS and Android), but we want to use a new, promising channel to increase sales.

7. Assumptions / expectations / mandatory elements

- The application should enable browsing ready pizzas and inform about prices
- The application will allow you to buy pizza in one of three sizes: small, medium or family
- The application should enable the purchase of more than one pizza
- The application is to enable the purchase of more than one type of pizza
- The application is to enable the purchase of pizza toppings (drink, sauces)
- The application is to enable placing an order in the place indicated by the customer and making payment for it
- The application is to enable order tracking (unlike using notifications)
- The application is to enable viewing historical orders
- The application is to enable the ordering of your favorite pizza with "one click"
- The application is to send information about subsequent order statuses (placed order, confirmed order, order in delivery)
- The application is to display information about the current order on the main screen
- The application will not require additional registrations in external systems (Pizza Hut system)
- The customer's name and surname will be from watchOS or Android and will be provided by the system
- Customer location data will come from GPS or entered
- Payments will be made based on the GPay system (for Android) or ApplePay (for iOS), no need to integrate with external systems
- The application is not intended to use voice commands
